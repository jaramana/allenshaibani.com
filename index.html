Allen Shaibani

Urban planner and emergency management professional working at the intersection of city systems, resilience, and risk.

Allen Shaibani is a senior urban planner and emergency management professional whose work centers on how cities anticipate, absorb, and recover from risk. In New York City government he led the development of the 2024 Hazard Mitigation Plan, a citywide resilience framework coordinating more than thirty agencies, directing a $1.1M effort and a team of seven staff and three consultants.

His earlier roles span the public, civic, and design sectors: accessibility and transportation planning at the NYC Taxi and Limousine Commission, and work with the Gehl Institute, the AIA New York Center for Architecture, and Eparque Urban Strategies. A graduate of Pratt Institute, his practice is informed by work abroad and a longstanding interest in how migration, community, and urban form shape one another.

He is currently pursuing consulting work at the intersection of urban resilience, planning, and city systems.
